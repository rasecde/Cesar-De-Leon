Intro

Hello! My name is Cesar De Leon, a Dominican American and first-generation college student from the Bronx, New York. My passion lies in computer science, particularly AI and machine learning. As an enthusiast of both technology and community, I strive to bridge gaps and contribute to a more inclusive future. Beyond coding, you'll find me playing the piano, cheering for the Notre Dame cheer team, and enjoying video games. Let's connect and explore the endless possibilities at the intersection of technology and diversity.

Work

Analyzed the Human-Pretending component of the PERPLEX (Prompt Engineering and Responsible Psychometric Learning for Explainable AI) study which aims to optimize generative AI prompts within the context of psychometrics to detect mental health problems through Large Language Models.

Tested the fine-tuning parameter specifications of LLMs like ChatGPT to accurately portray neuropsychiatric disorders by designing and evaluating simulated conversations using LLM adaptions of Clinically validated assessment tools.

Implemented some of the simulated conversations through a Python environment using ChatGPT's API in order to automate the simulations and allow for better efficiency.

About

My name is Cesar De Leon, a tech nerd with a journey as diverse as the communities I've been a part of. While I was born in Manhattan, New York, I spent my early childhood in the Dominican Republic, embracing its culture before returning to the vibrant streets of the Bronx, New York. Of course, when I returned, I had to learn English and adopt some American customs, which I am grateful for as it has provided me with a unique perspective that makes up who I am today.

For me, it all began in the world of gaming, particularly in platforms like Roblox, which helped me “Power” my imagination. Experimenting with game creation in Roblox, modding applications and games on my phone, and playing around with game scripts sparked an interest in technology within me. As I gained more formal experience with programming though programs, clubs, and classes in highschool, I eventually decided that I wanted to pursue a degree in computer science.

Fast forward to today, and I find myself living under the Golden Dome at University of Notre Dame, where I'm pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science with a minor in Engineering Corporate Practice. During my time here so far, I’ve made a college family and have had many enriching experiences, from studying abroad in Dublin to contributing to transformative initiatives like the Transformational Leaders Program and the Notre Dame Family Volunteer Camp.

Beyond academics, I enjoy participating in a variety of hobbies and extracurriculars. This may include cheering for the Fighting Irish as a member of the Notre Dame Cheer Team, programming and test-driving robots for the Robotic Football Club, playing the piano in my dorm’s chapel during my own time, or weightlifting and practicing Calisthenics in the gym.

However, my journey is not just about personal achievements; it's also about fostering inclusivity and driving positive change. Growing up in the projects in The Bronx opened my eyes to some of the struggles that many people from underrepresented backgrounds like myself face on a day-to-day basis. The only difference being that I was fortunate enough to receive the opportunity to pursue higher education. From my involvement with the New Visions Student Advisory Network in the Bronx to leading logistics for the Latino First-Year Retreat at Notre Dame, I'm committed to bridging gaps and empowering communities.

With my never-ending curiosity, I am driven by a pursuit of knowledge and a desire to make a difference, and so I embrace each opportunity as a chance to learn, grow, and connect. As I continue to navigate the dynamic intersection of technology, diversity, and community, I invite you to join me on this journey of exploration and impact.

Let's connect, collaborate, and explore the endless possibilities that lie ahead.
